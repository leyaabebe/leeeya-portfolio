crunchyroll

  

website redesign

abstract

How can crunchyroll be redesigned to better serve its audience?

By conducting research on website performance and users, I have targeted areas within the crunchyroll experience that could be improved in flow, interaction, and appearance. I will walk you through my process starting with research.


role

user research, user surveys, wireframing, and visual design


duration

8 weeks


tools

paper, sketch


links

visit prototype

download case study

research

A usability evaluation reveals multiple areas for improvement.



usability

  • Navigation doesn't travel with scroll
  • Requires a lot of scrolling to reach top
  • Content is tightly packed on the page
  • Related content not in close proximity
  • Advertisements don't have CTAs
accessibility

  • Needs more text alternatives to non-text content (e.g. images, videos)
  • Not fully keyboard accessible
  • Headings and labels use similar sizing
  • Repetitive naming of text links
  • Instances of very low color contrast
  • Doesn't have a site map
functionality

  • There are broken and misnamed links
  • Site crashes frequently on web, mobile, and other platforms
presentation
  • Branding, layout, and text styling is fairly consistent on all pages except: forums, store, premium, log in, random, individual videos, search results, platforms and devices, and about.

research

A high visitor count butts heads with a poor bounce rate; Home and search are the most visited pages.

I found statistics on how users behave when they visit crunchyroll as of January 2019. Even though the number of visitors is on the rise and for all the content offered, nearly half of those visitors immediately leave and the average amount of pages click through nears 4 per visitor.

The United States make up 42.3% of traffic on crunchyroll and is the main country of focus in this redesign. Traffic usually comes directly from the site at 65.59% with search second at 22.23%.

competitive analysis

Many channels exist to stream anime; so, why choose crunchyroll over them? What's unique?

Users seek out websites that provide some convenience in watchig nanime. How that is perceived varies from: being able to download episodes, to watch episodes for free but with advertisements, or to watch episodes without advertisements but for a fee

To help users decide whether paying for membership is worth it, they often consider the convenience along with other features provided by the service. The table to the right gives an overview of features offered by CR and its competitors.

user surveys

Survey results placed Netflix first at 31% viewership and sites for pirated content aggregated at 35% as major competitors.

To better understand how people watch anime and what they expect from those websites in regards to features, I conducted a survey through google forms to learn more.

The survey is still open, but as of April 25th, respondents were mainly millennials, female, students, living inthe Unitd States, and have received a graduate education. A majority do watch anime and use sites that weren't all expected; of those sites written in, collectively they made up 18% of preffered viewing.

target audience

CR users are divided among Premium and Normal members; this redesign focuses on those who use CR regularly.


Why watch CR as a premium member?
  • To watch episodes without advertisements
  • To watch anime ahead of others
  • To get discounts to events and merchandise
  • To help the platform because of its mission
  • All of the Normal member benefits included
Why watch Cr as a Normal member?
  • Visual appeal is better than most competitors
  • Ability to interact with the community
  • More anime, manga, anime news, events, etc., offered than competitors
  • Accessible across many devices and languages
